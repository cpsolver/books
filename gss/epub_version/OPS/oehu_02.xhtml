Scene 1, Our Middle Class Dreams

Katerina: Damien! Are you thinking of jumping off?

Damien: With today's luck I'd land on something soft.

Katerina: Into a vat of fat? Or onto a truckload of over-ripe pumpkins?

Damien: Or on my head.

Katerina: That would put a dent in paying next month's rent. What's the matter?

Damien: I'm (slurred first word) feelin' sadder than a turtle with no shell.

Katerina: Why so pitiful? [abysmal?]

Damien: My dream's been shattered.

Katerina: Which dream? The one about a picket fence around a front yard big enough for a Saint Bernard?

Damien: That's your dream. Mine was to enter the middle class.

Katerina: Yeah, to gain that entrance you've been bustin' your ass.

Damien: My butt is busted. What more does it take?

Katerina: Maybe a secret handshake?

Damien: Today my boss popped my bubble.

Katerina: Is his business in trouble?

Damien: Nah, but he told me some stuff, about some laws that are rough.

Katerina: The ones corporations can avoid?

Damien: Yeah, that's got me annoyed.

Katerina: Some corporations are more corrupt than those traveling medicine shows of long ago.

Damien: And us taxpayers get pushed to the edge of a volcano looking down at the molten lava called bankruptcy.

Katerina: You didn't tell me we're close to bankruptcy. How can this be?

Damien: How is it that those of us who vote are not the ones keeping afloat? We're supposed to have democracy!

Katerina: Yes we can vote, but in a general election the only choices are con artists and wimpy puppets. And marking the oval next to any name tickles my nose with a sulferous stink.

Damien: How can that be? That's a big kink in democracy.

Katerina: Well it does seem crazy. Laws always favor the wealthy.

Damien: Yet they are fewer in number than rabbits behind the path of a coyote pack.

Katerina: And there are so many of us, making a big fuss.

Damien: How can the rich outvote us? Is the only prognosis hopelessness?

Katerina: Maybe it's hypnosis? Or the political ads I suppose.

Damien: We can see through those. Something's hidden. There's got to be another villian.

Katerina: A conspiracy? A dark-money tyranny? Or a Bermuda pentagon with two sides missing?

Damien: I don't know. But as my dad would say, "Get to the root of the problem, that's where the solution lay."

Katerina: My parents, they taught me, suit up, show up, pay attention, and then work very hard, forever.

Damien: Willing to bust my ass, that I'm willin' to do. But not if this is the sequel to Native Americans proclaiming "The white man are like wolves who herd us like buffalo from our grazing lands into arid regions. They take our lands to fuel their greed beyond their need."

Katerina: I agree, something's not right.

Damien: Damn I want to know whose hand I should bite!

Katerina: Financially, our life is so far upside down from where I want it to be.

Damien: I wanna be an employer, but I'm just an employee.

Katerina: I wanna be a princess. But do you see a tiara on my empty head?

Damien: We are upside down. It's not our nose that should run. It's our feet that should run.

Katerina: It's not our feet that should smell. It's our nose that should smell.

Damien: We are upside down. Our finances are in the red, not in the black.

Katerina: If our dinner plate has roasted veggies and some meat, then our car's gas gauge says stay at home this weekend.

Damien: If our teeth are clean and worthy of good cheer, then at dinner out with friends we'll do without the beer.

Katerina: Do you want a chocolate truffle?

Damien: Something brown to get me down, no thanks.

Katerina: They're delicious.

Damien: I'm suspicious. Something's going on behind our backs.

Katerina: Yes, right in front of our eyes. The trickle-down economy works for corporations, but not for us.

Damien: Work and cash should come as a pair, but one without the other, that's not fair!

Katerina: We're stuck doing dull work for bosses that are jerks.

Damien: Luckily the boss I've got is a saint compared to others. But now he says he's drowning. There's taxes and licenses and insurance and yet more fees. Just keeping track of these adds another employee.

Katerina: That leaves less money for us.

Damien: I know how to invent but that don't pay the rent. Not yet. And it won't if I can't figure out how to own my own business.

Katerina: What are you going to do?

Damien: I want to set things right, so I'll just have to fight. But who? Who do I fight?

Katerina: We've now got internet communication and computers. So that should give us liberation.

Damien: Or at least an answer to who we should fight.

Katerina: What can you do?

Damien: Surely somewhere there is someone who's wise. But a Google search won't be enough 'cause those ads are from the highest bidders, the very same ones who steal from us.

Katerina: The ones who want to take away our take-home pay. Yet what would you ask if you found a guru who knew?

Damien: What's at the root of unfairness in business and politics?

Katerina: The local college has professors who are bright. Some know about political science. They teach about electoral college and gerrymandation, er, German dating, or whatever, and the Constitution.

Damien: I'd rather walk into a rattlesnake cage than open another textbook. They shake my eyes more than the snake's tail. And my brain throbs when it tries to follow where the weaselly words try to drag me. And tests, those devils just try to figure out if you read the textbook. Now that's a real conspiracy.

Katerina: I have to agree. Textbooks and tests are in it together.

Damien: Just being inside a classroom feels like I've stepped into a boxing ring wearing not boxing gloves but holding pom poms.

Katerina: Ah yes, I forgot that community college classes knocked you out for the count.

Damien: Does it matter? History is just about the past that's gone.

Katerina: Just like Elvis. I admit I don't remember much.

Damien: My math teacher said a monkey with a bad resume could have done better on his tests. And fractions were created by the devil!

Katerina: Alas you have to deal with evil fractions every day at work.

Damien: I cry inside when I get a measurement like thirteen and five sixteenths inches.

Katerina: I wouldn't know what to do with that.

Damien: Why should I struggle in the academic jungle?

Katerina: I admit I don't recall much at all from classes I took.

Damien: Anyway, do political scientists understand why politics is so upside down? With the rich on top n' us voters stuck in the swamp of financial disadvantage?

Katerina: Do we even really have democracy?

Damien: Partial democracy, yes. But it's not a full democracy. I long to figure out how it is that the majority of voters get outvoted by dollars from the wallets of the top one percent.

Katerina: I'll ask father. He might know someone who knows those secrets.

Damien: I know I'm not the sharpest banana in the fruit basket. But if there should be a really sharp banana who can tell me, without pointing to some heavy book, why the world is so upside down, then I'll stuggle to learn those secrets.

Katerina: And when you find out who's to blame for corruption in Washington DC, what comes next?

Damien: Then I'll know how to beat the system. Then we can earn the money we deserve for working so hard for the masters we serve.

Katerina: I've read that suicide is done by men who can't find anyone else to blame. So if you don't figure this out, are you going to jump?

Damien: I won't jump. I'm not a chump. When the going gets tough, I'll jump back on the horse and keep on ridin'. 'Cause I'm dyin'. To know why things are upside down.

Katerina: As long as you're not dyin' on me.

Damien: Some people say this is just the way it will always be. But that's not OK for me. It isn't fair! Getting ahead. Rising to the top. Why does it seem to happen just for jerks and blowhards?

Katerina: Yes, us hard workers deserve some perks too, don't we?

Damien: Seats in Congress should go to the wise.

Katerina: Yet the winners are always liars.

Damien: And only the greedy survive in that cesspool. Us voters want to elect problem-solving leaders. But what do we get? Special-interest puppets. They do all sorts of bidding for the wealthy few. The top one percent.

Katerina: Like the cream that floats to the top of raw milk.

Damien: And what do they get?

Katerina: Even richer!

Damien: And what do the rest of us get?

Katerina: Treated like a light bulb. Screwed!

Damien: I want to change the world and turn it right side up!

Katerina: The impossible. That's part of why I love you so. You want things to to be fair. You really care! But you'll need lots and lots of luck.

Damien: Yet with it I'll discover a way out of this muck.

Katerina: And then finally we can reach prosperity.

Damien: And suffer less adversity.

Katerina: I'll ask father for some help.

Damien: And I will use my soft brain as a shovel to dig deep into the elephant and donkey dung! My sanity depends on it.
