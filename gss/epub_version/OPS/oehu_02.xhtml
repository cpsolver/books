Scene 1, Our Middle Class Dreams

Katerina: Damien! Are you thinking of jumping off?

Damien: With today's luck I'd land on something soft.

Katerina: Like a truck full of pumpkins? Or a fresh load of horse manure?

Damien: Or my head.

Katerina: That would put a dent in paying next month's rent. What's the matter?

Damien: I'm (slurred first word) feelin' sadder than a deaf seagull.

Katerina: Why so abysmal?

Damien: Life's so unfair. My dream's been shattered.

Katerina: Which dream's been fractured? The picket fence around a front yard that's big enough for a Saint Bernard?

Damien: That's your dream. Mine was to enter the middle class.

Katerina: Yeah, to gain that entrance you've been bustin' your ass.

Damien: My butt is busted. What more does it take?

Katerina: Maybe a secret handshake?

Damien: Today my boss popped my bubble.

Katerina: Is his business in trouble?

Damien: Nah, but he told me some stuff, about some laws that are rough.

Katerina: The ones corporations can avoid?

Damien: Yeah, that's got me annoyed.

Katerina: Some corporations are sneakier than pickpockets, drug dealers, and kids giving away free kittens.

Damien: Us taxpayers get pushed to the edge. Don't they see? We're looking down into a volcano of molten lava called bankruptcy.

Katerina: You didn't tell me we're close to the edge. How can this be?

Damien: How is it that we who vote aren't the ones keepin' afloat? We're supposed to have democracy!

Katerina: Yes we can vote, but most candidates are wimpy puppets, or slimey con artists.

Damien: Or third-party spoilers. I hate that sulfurous stink when I have to mark the lesser fink.

Katerina: Yeah it's a big kink in democracy. Laws always favor the wealthy.

Damien: We make a big fuss, yet the rich outvote us.

Katerina: Even though they're fewer in number than rabbits near a den of coyotes.

Damien: Is the only prognosis hopelessness?

Katerina: Maybe it's hypnosis? Or the political ads I suppose.

Damien: We can see through those. Something's hidden. There's got to be another villian.

Katerina: A conspiracy? A dark-money tyranny? Or a Bermuda pentagon with two sides missing?

Damien: I dunno. But as my dad would say, "Get to the root of the problem, that's where the solution lay."

Katerina: My parents, they taught me, suit up, show up, pay attention, and then pretend to work work work.

Damien: Willing to bust my ass, that I'm willin' to do. But not if we're following the footsteps of Native Americans.

Katerina: Footsteps?

Damien: They said "White chiefs are like wolves who herd us like buffalo, in winter through snow, from our grazing lands onto dry deserts where foods don't grow. They steal our lands to fuel their greed beyond their need."

Katerina: We're being herded indeed. Something's not right.

Damien: Damn I want to know whose hand I should bite!

Katerina: Financially, our life is so far upside down from where I want it to be.

Damien: I wanna be an employer, but I'm just an employee.

Katerina: I wanna be a princess. But do you see a tiara on my empty head?

Damien: Yeah, we're upside down. It lets money drain out of our pockets. Our finances are in the red, but they should be black instead.

Katerina: If my dental smile gives us cheer, at dinner out we can't afford beer.

Damien: If our dinner's got veggies and meat, our car's gas gauge says stay off the street.

Katerina: Do you want a chocolate truffle?

Damien: Something brown to get me down, no thanks.

Katerina: They're delicious.

Damien: I'm suspicious. Something's going on behind our backs.

Katerina: Yes, right in front of our eyes. The trickle-down economy works for corporations, but not for us.

Damien: Work and cash should come as a pair, but one without the other, that's not effing fair!

Katerina: Yeah, we're indentured to jerks and getting only a few small perks.

Damien: The boss I've got's almost a saint. But now he says he's drownin'. There's taxes and licenses, and insurance, and extra fees. Just keepin' track of these adds another employee.

Katerina: That leaves less money for me. Your paycheck's like a balloon with a leak.

Damien: Times are bleak. I know how to invent but that don't pay the rent. Not yet. And it won't if I'm not victorious owning my own business.

Katerina: I've read that suicide is done by guys who can't find anyone else to blame. So who do you think has rigged the political game?

Damien: I dunno. Politics's so complex.

Katerina: Yeah, everything interconnects.

Damien: Yet surely somewhere, maybe sitting in a yoga pose on a high mountaintop, or kneeling at an altar in a cathedral, or standing behind a counter asking "Do you want cheezy garlic bread with your pasta?", there's just gotta be a geek, or geekette, who knows the secret.

Katerina: Even Google can't find that needle in the online haystack.

Damien: 'Specially 'cause most online search has kickback associations with big corporations.

Katerina: Let's just say you found a guru who knew. What would you ask, specifically?

Damien: How can we get better democracy? Wait, not that. I'd ask "What's at the root of unfairness in politics and business?"

Katerina: Bright professors teach at our city's college. In political science they teach about the electrical college, and German dating, er, gerrymandation, and such.

Damien: I'd rather walk into a rattlesnake cage than turn another textbook page. Those pages shake my eyes faster than the snake's tail. My brain throbs as weaselly words try to drag me through names and dates, and definitions. Plus facts I'll never use. And tests, those effing devils, just rat out those of us who didn't read the textbook.

Katerina: Now that's a real conspiracy. Textbooks and tests are in bed together.

Damien: Just being inside a classroom feels like I've stepped into a boxing ring wearing not boxing gloves but holding pom poms.

Katerina: Ah yes, community college classes already knocked you out for the count.

Damien: Does it matter? History's just fat with notes from the past that's gone.

Katerina: Just like an Elvis museum.

Damien: My math teacher said a monkey with a bad resume could have done better on his tests. And fractions, those were created by the devil when he was in a snit!

Damien: Yeah, I admit, I struggled a bit in the academic jungle. I tip-toed, and side-stepped, and a few times I tripped.

Damien: Anyway, political scientists, do they really understand why politics's so perverted that it's inverted. Surely, if they knew, we wouldn't be stuck in this financial muck.

Katerina: I suppose you're right.

Damien: Who should I fight? Where's that thing, the tilting place?

Katerina: The tipping point?

Damien: Yeah, how can we, the majority, get to the point where we can outvote the endless dollars of one percenters?

Katerina: Father knows some jet setters from a distance. I'll ask him for assistance.

Damien: I know I'm not the sharpest banana in the fruit basket. But if there should be a really sharp banana who can tell me, without pointing to some heavy book, why the world's so upside down, then I'll stuggle to learn those secrets.

Katerina: And when you find out who's to blame for corruption in the governmental game, what comes next?

Damien: Then I'll shift from working hard for the masters we serve to earning the money we deserve.

Katerina: If you don't get out of your slump, are you going to jump?

Damien: I won't jump. I'm not a chump. It might take awhile, but I'll jump back on the crocodile and keep on ridin'. 'Cause I'm dyin'. To know why things are upside down.

Katerina: As long as you're not dyin' on me.

Damien: Some people say this is just the way it will always be. But it's not OK. It's not fair! Getting ahead. Rising to the top. Why does it seem to happen just for jerks and windbags?

Katerina: Yes, us hard workers deserve some perks too.

Damien: Seats in Congress should go to the wise.

Katerina: Yet elections are won by selfish liars.

Damien: We want problem-solving leaders. What d' we get?

Katerina: Money-controlled special-interest puppets.

Damien: What d' the puppet-masters get?

Katerina: Even richer!

Damien: And the rest of us get what?

Katerina: Treated like a light bulb. Screwed!

Damien: I wanna change the world and turn it right side up!

Katerina: The impossible. That's part of why I love you so. You want things to to be fair. You really care! But you'll need lots and lots of luck.

Damien: Yet with it I hope to discover a way out of this muck.

Katerina: Then finally we can reach prosperity.

Damien: And suffer less adversity.

Katerina: I'll ask father for assistance.

Damien: And I, the dunce, will use my soft brain. As a shovel to dig down deep into elephant and donkey shit! My sanity depends on it.
