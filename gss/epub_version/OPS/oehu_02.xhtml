Scene 1, Our Middle Class Dreams

Katerina: Damien! Are you thinking of jumping off?

Damien: Wi' today's luck I'd land on somethin' soft.

Katerina: Like a fresh load of horse manure? Or a truck full of marshmallows?

Damien: Or my head.

Katerina: That would put a dent ... in paying next month's rent. What's the matter?

Damien: Ah'm feelin' sadder than a limpin' seagull in winter in Seattle.

Katerina: How'd you get there? Why so abysmal?

Damien: Life's so unfair. M' dream's been shattered.

Katerina: Which dream's been fractured? The picket fence around a front yard that's big enough for a Saint Bernard?

Damien: Tha's your dream. M'n was to enter th' middle class.

Katerina: Yeah, to gain that entrance you've been bustin' your ass.

Damien: M' butt's busted. Wha' more does it take?

Katerina: Maybe a secret handshake? What's up?

Damien: T'day my boss popped my bubble.

Katerina: Is his business in trouble?

Damien: Nah, bu' he told me some stuff, 'bout some laws that're rough.

Katerina: The ones corporations can avoid?

Damien: Yeah, it's got me annoyed.

Katerina: Some corporations are sneakier than pickpockets, drug dealers, and kids giving away free kittens.

Damien: Us taxpayers get pushed t' the edge. Don't they see? We're lookin' down into a volcano of molten lava called bankruptcy.

Katerina: You didn't tell me we're close to the edge. How can this be?

Damien: 'Ow is't we who vote aren't th' ones keepin' afloat? W're supposed t' have d'mocracy!

Katerina: Yes we can vote, but most candidates are wimpy puppets, or slimey con artists.

Damien: 'R third-party spoilers. I hate tha' sulfurous stink when I hav'ta mark th' lesser fink.

Katerina: Yeah it's a big kink in democracy. Laws always favor the wealthy.

Damien: We make a big fuss, yet th' rich outvote us.

Katerina: Yet they're fewer in number than rabbits near a den of coyotes.

Damien: Is th' only prognosis hopelessness?

Katerina: Maybe it's hypnosis? Or the political ads I suppose.

Damien: We c'n see through those. Somethin's hidden. There's gotta be another villian.

Katerina: A conspiracy? A dark-money tyranny? Or a Bermuda pentagon with two sides missing?

Damien: I dunno. Bu' as my dad would say, Get to the root of the problem, that's where the solution lay.

Katerina: My parents, they taught me, Suit up, show up, pay attention, and then pretend to work work work.

Damien: Willin' to bust my ass, that I'm willin' t' do. Bu' not if we're followin' th' footsteps of Native Americans.

Katerina: What footsteps?

Damien: They spoke White chiefs are like wolves who herd us like buffalo. From our grazing lands onto dry deserts where foods don't grow. Those two-faced wolves feed their greed beyond their need.

Katerina: We're bein' herded indeed. Something's not right.

Damien: Damn I wanna know whose hand I should bite!

Katerina: Financially, our life's so far upside down from where I want it to be.

Damien: I wanna be an employer, but I'm just an employee.

Katerina: I wanna be a princess. But do you see a tiara on my empty head?

Damien: Yeah, we're upside down. Money's drainin' from our pockets like chips from a novice at a Los Vegas craps table.

Katerina: My dental smile gives us cheer, but now when dining out we can't afford beer.

Damien: When dinner's got veggies 'n meat, our car's gas gauge says stay off th' street.

Katerina: Do you want a chocolate truffle?

Damien: Somethin' brown t' get me down, no thanks.

Katerina: They're delicious.

Damien: I'm suspicious. Something's goin' on behind our backs.

Katerina: Yes, right in front of our eyes. The trickle-down economy works for corporations, but not for us.

Damien: Work 'n cash should come as a pair, but one without th' other, that's not effin' fair!

Katerina: Yeah, we're indentured to jerks and getting only pea-sized perks.

Damien: Th' boss I've got's almost a saint. Bu' now he says he's drownin'. There's taxes 'n licenses, 'n insurance, 'n extra fees. Jus' keepin' track of these adds another employee.

Katerina: That leaves less money for me. Your paycheck's like a balloon with a leak.

Damien: Times are bleak. I know how to invent but that don't pay the rent. Not yet. N' it won't if I'm not victorious owning my own business.

Katerina: I've read that suicide is done by guys who can't find anyone else to blame. So who do you think's rigged th' political game?

Damien: I dunno. Politics's so complex.

Katerina: Yeah, everything interconnects.

Damien: Yet surely somewhere, maybe sittin' in a yoga pose on a high mountaintop, or kneelin' at an altar in a gothic cathedral, or standin' behind a counter asking Do you want cheezy garlic bread with your pasta?, there's just gotta be a geek, or geekette, who knows the secret.

Katerina: Even Google can't find that needle in th' cyberweb haystack.

Damien: 'Specially 'cause most online search has kickback associations with big corporations.

Katerina: Let's just say you found a guru who knew. What'd be your query, specifically?

Damien: How can we get better democracy? Wait, not that. I'd ask What's at the root of unfairness in politics and business?

Katerina: Bright professors teach at our city's college. In political science they teach about the electrical college, and German dating, er, gerrymandation, and such.

Damien: I'd rather walk into a rattlesnake cage than turn another textbook page. Those pages shake my eyes faster than th' snake's tail. My brain throbs as weaselly words try t' drag me through names 'n dates, 'n definitions. Plus facts I'll never use. 'N tests, those effin' devils, just rat out those of us who didn't read th' textbook.

Katerina: Now that's a real conspiracy. Textbooks and tests are in bed together, making baby test questions.

Damien: Jus' bein' inside a classroom feels like I've stepped into a boxing ring wearing not boxing gloves but holding pom poms.

Katerina: Ah yes, community college classes already knocked you out for the count.

Damien: D'z it matter? History's just fat with notes from the past that's gone.

Katerina: Just like an Elvis museum.

Damien: M' math teacher said a monkey with a bad resume could have done better on his tests. N' fractions, those were created by the devil when he was in a snit!

Damien: Yeah, I admit, I struggled a bit in the academic jungle. I tip-toed, and side-stepped, and a few times I tripped.

Damien: Anyway, political scientists, do they really understand why politics's so perverted, so inverted? Surely, if they knew, then Congress would better the economy, and we wouldn't be stuck in this financial muck.

Katerina: I suppose you're right.

Damien: Who should I fight? Where's that thing, the tilting place?

Katerina: The tipping point?

Damien: Yeah, how can we, the majority, get to where we can outvote the endless dollars of one percenters?

Katerina: From a distance father knows some jet setters. I'll ask him for assistance.

Damien: I know I'm not the sharpest banana in the fruit basket. Bu' if there should be a really sharp banana who can tell me, without pointing to some heavy book, why the world's so upside down, then I'll stuggle to learn, and then turn things right side up.

Katerina: And when you find out who's to blame for corruption in the governmental game, what comes next?

Damien: Then I'll shift from workin' hard for th' masters we serve t' earning th' money we deserve.

Katerina: If you don't get out of your slump, are you gonna jump?

Damien: I won't jump. I'm not a chump. 'T might take awhile, but I'll jump back on the crocodile and keep on ridin'. 'Cause I'm dyin'. T' know why things are upside down.

Katerina: As long as you're not dyin' on me.

Damien: S'm people say this is just th' way it will always be. Bu' it's not OK. It's not fair! Seats 'n Congress should go to the wise. Yet sitting there now are selfish liars. We wanna elect problem-solving leaders, bu' what d' we get? Egotistic special-interest puppets whose masters treat us like light bulbs.

Katerina: Yeah, screwed.

Damien: I wanna change th' world n' turn it right side up!

Katerina: The impossible. That's part of why I love you so. You want things to to be fair. You really care! But you'll need lots and lots of luck.

Damien: Yet with it I hope t' discover a way outta this muck.

Katerina: Then finally we can reach prosperity.

Damien: 'N suffer less adversity.

Katerina: I'll ask father for assistance.

Damien: 'N I, the dunce, will use my soft brain. As a shovel to dig down deep into elephant and donkey shit! M' sanity depends on it.
