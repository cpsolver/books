Scene 1, Our Middle Class Dreams

Katerina: Damien! Are you thinking of jumping off?

Damien: With today's luck I'd land on something soft.

Katerina: Like a truckload of pumpkins? Or a cart of dung?

Damien: Or my head.

Katerina: That would put a dent in paying next month's rent. What's the matter?

Damien: I'm (slurred first word) feelin' sadder than a dolphin with no fin.

Katerina: Named Adolf? Why so abysmal?

Damien: Life's so unfair. My dream's been shattered.

Katerina: Which dream's been fractured? The picket fence around a front yard that's big enough for a Saint Bernard?

Damien: That's your dream. Mine was to enter the middle class.

Katerina: Yeah, to gain that entrance you've been bustin' your ass.

Damien: My butt is busted. What more does it take?

Katerina: Maybe a secret handshake?

Damien: Today my boss popped my bubble.

Katerina: Is his business in trouble?

Damien: Nah, but he told me some stuff, about some laws that are rough.

Katerina: The ones corporations can avoid?

Damien: Yeah, that's got me annoyed.

Katerina: Some corporations are more corrupt than those long-ago traveling medicine shows.

Damien: And us taxpayers get pushed to the edge. We're looking down into a volcano of molten lava called bankruptcy.

Katerina: You didn't tell me we're close to the edge. How can this be?

Damien: How is it that we who vote aren't the ones keepin' afloat? We're supposed to have democracy!

Katerina: Yes we can vote, but most candidates are wimpy puppets, or con artists, or can't-win third-party whiners.

Damien: Marking the ballot oval for any such fink fills m' nose with a sulferous stink.

Katerina: Yeah it's a big kink in democracy.

Damien: How can this be?

Katerina: It does seem crazy. Laws always favor the wealthy.

Damien: But they're fewer in number than rabbits behind the path of a coyote pack.

Katerina: While so many of us make a big fuss.

Damien: How is it the rich outvote us? Is the only prognosis hopelessness?

Katerina: Maybe it's hypnosis? Or the political ads I suppose.

Damien: We can see through those. Something's hidden. There's got to be another villian.

Katerina: A conspiracy? A dark-money tyranny? Or a Bermuda pentagon with two sides missing?

Damien: I dunno. But as my dad would say, "Get to the root of the problem, that's where the solution lay."

Katerina: My parents, they taught me, suit up, show up, pay attention, and then pretend to work work work.

Damien: Willing to bust my ass, that I'm willin' to do. But not if we're following the footsteps of Native Americans.

Katerina: Footsteps?

Damien: They said "The white man are like wolves who herd us like buffalo from our grazing lands onto deserts. They steal our lands to fuel their greed beyond their need."

Katerina: We're being herded indeed. Something's not right.

Damien: Damn I want to know whose hand I should bite!

Katerina: Financially, our life is so far upside down from where I want it to be.

Damien: I wanna be an employer, but I'm just an employee.

Katerina: I wanna be a princess. But do you see a tiara on my empty head?

Damien: We're upside down. It's not our nose that should run. Our feet should run.

Katerina: It's not our feet that should smell. Our nose should smell.

Damien: We're upside down. Our finances are in the red, not black.

Katerina: If our teeth's appearance gives us cheer, at dinner out we can't afford beer.

Damien: If our dinner's got veggies and meat, our car's gas gauge says stay off the street.

Katerina: Do you want a chocolate truffle?

Damien: Something brown to get me down, no thanks.

Katerina: They're delicious.

Damien: I'm suspicious. Something's going on behind our backs.

Katerina: Yes, right in front of our eyes. The trickle-down economy works for corporations, but not for us.

Damien: Work and cash should come as a pair, but one without the other, that's not effing fair!

Katerina: Yeah, we're stuck working for jerks doing work that sucks.

Damien: The boss I've got's almost a saint. But now he says he's drownin'. There's taxes and licenses, and insurance, and extra fees. Just keepin' track of these adds another employee.

Katerina: That leaves less money for us.

Damien: I know how to invent but that don't pay the rent. Not yet. And it won't if I can't figure out how to own my own business.

Katerina: I've read that suicide is done by men who can't find anyone else to blame. So who is rigging the political game?

Damien: I don't know. Politics's so complex.

Katerina: Yeah, everything interconnects.

Damien: Yet surely somewhere, maybe in an old cathedral, or on a high mountaintop, or in a dive bar, someone really smart knows the secret.

Katerina: Even Google can't find that needle in the global haystack.

Damien: 'Specially 'cause online networks kick us back to the money-backed asswipes.

Katerina: Yeah, the evil ones who rip us off, leaving less take-home pay. Let's just say you found a guru who knew. What would you ask, specifically?

Damien: How can we get real democracy? Wait, not that. I'd ask "What's at the root of unfairness in politics and business?"

Katerina: Bright professors teach at our city's college. In political science they teach about the Constitution, and electrical college, and German dating, er, gerrymandation, and such.

Damien: I'd rather walk into a rattlesnake cage than open another textbook. They shake my eyes faster than the snake's tail. My brain throbs as weaselly words try to drag me through names and dates, and definitions, and facts I'll never use. And tests, those effing devils, just rat out those of us who didn't read the textbook.

Katerina: Now that's a real conspiracy. Textbooks and tests are in bed together.

Damien: Just being inside a classroom feels like I've stepped into a boxing ring wearing not boxing gloves but holding pom poms.

Katerina: Ah yes, community college classes already knocked you out for the count.

Damien: Does it matter? History's just about the past that's gone.

Katerina: Just like Elvis. I admit I don't remember much.

Damien: My math teacher said a monkey with a bad resume could have done better on his tests. And fractions, those were created by the devil!

Katerina: Alas you have to deal with evil fractions every day at work.

Damien: I cry inside when I get a measurement like thirteen and five sixteenths inches.

Katerina: I wouldn't know what to do with that.

Damien: Why should I struggle in the academic jungle?

Katerina: I don't recall much at all from classes I took.

Damien: Anyway, do political scientists understand why politics's so upside down? With rich on top n' us voters mucking around in a financial swamp.

Katerina: I suppose you're right.

Damien: Who should I fight? Where's the tipping point? How can we, the majority, outvote the slush funds of one percenters?

Katerina: I'll ask father. He might know someone who knows those secrets.

Damien: I know I'm not the sharpest banana in the fruit basket. But if there should be a really sharp banana who can tell me, without pointing to some heavy book, why the world's so upside down, then I'll stuggle to learn those secrets.

Katerina: And when you find out who's to blame for corruption in government, what comes next?

Damien: Then I'll know how to earn the money we deserve. For working so hard for the masters we serve.

Katerina: If you don't figure this out, are you going to jump?

Damien: I won't jump. I'm not a chump. When the going gets tough, I'll jump back on the crocodile and keep on ridin'. 'Cause I'm dyin'. To know why things are upside down.

Katerina: As long as you're not dyin' on me.

Damien: Some people say this is just the way it will always be. But that's not OK for me. It's not fair! Getting ahead. Rising to the top. Why does it seem to happen just for jerks and windbags?

Katerina: Yes, us hard workers deserve some perks too, don't we?

Damien: Seats in Congress should go to the wise.

Katerina: Yet the winners are selfish liars.

Damien: We want problem-solving leaders. What d' we get?

Katerina: Special-interest puppets.

Damien: What d' the puppet-masters get?

Katerina: Even richer!

Damien: And the rest of us get what?

Katerina: Treated like a light bulb. Screwed!

Damien: I wanna change the world and turn it right side up!

Katerina: The impossible. That's part of why I love you so. You want things to to be fair. You really care! But you'll need lots and lots of luck.

Damien: Yet with it I hope to discover a way out of this muck.

Katerina: Then finally we can reach prosperity.

Damien: And suffer less adversity.

Katerina: I'll ask father for some help.

Damien: I'll use my soft brain as a shovel to dig deep into the elephant and donkey dung! My sanity depends on it.
