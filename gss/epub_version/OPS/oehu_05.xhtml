Scene 4, Fighting Against Darkness

Desha: [Sitting on a stone bench in a cemetery eating a cookie.] You're way late. And don't call this a date.

Damien: 'R you Desha?

Desha: Who else would be in this spot in this cemetery at this time?

Damien: Nice t' meet ya. I'm Damien.

Desha: Of course.

Damien: Thanks f'r meetin' wi' me.

Desha: For nothing.

Damien: Huh?

Desha: That's the meaning of de nada in Spanish.

Damien: Yeah I know. M' mom's Puerto Rican.

Desha: Then why did you ask?

Damien: Is there cannibis in tha' cookie?

Desha: No you can't have one.

Damien: I jus' wondered what's in it.

Desha: Chocolate chips, dried cherries, oatmeal, stevia, sugar, and ghee, but nothing funny.

Damien: Ya said you wanted to meet wi' me.

Desha: Congressman Finckster said I should meet with you.

Damien: T' discuss why politics is so dysfunctional, y' said.

Desha: Damn right! It's so so so dysfunctional.

Damien: So, did he tell ya about vote splittin'?

Desha: What do goats have to do with politics?

Damien: Not goats. Not spitting. Vote splitting.

Desha: Splitting votes in half? That's a half-wit idea.

Damien: Not splitting ballots. Splitting voters.

Desha: That's murder. Yes politicians do murder innocent people by their flawed laws.

Damien: That's not what vote splitting is.

Desha: Then it sounds quite esoteric. What's important is that too many people are suffering under cruel corruption.

Damien: Yeah, corruption's so unfair. It's like leavin' just four squares o' toilet paper on'a toilet paper roll.

Desha: It's millions of times worse. Corruption kills through economic suffering that leads to suicide, homicide, overdoses, homelessness, and more. Corruption kills by allowing gun makers to push assault rifles into the hands of mentally unbalanced young males. And corruption kills pedestrians by giving preference to cars. And the companion of corruption is despair. Life is so unfair.

Damien: Th' companion of corruption is despair, if that's from th' Bible I've never heard it, bu' I agree.

Desha: Get to the point.

Damien: Vote splittin's at the root of unfair elections, so do ya wanna hear about it?

Desha: Tell me how you think it makes a difference.

Damien: Here's uh way to understand it. Suppose there's four politicians.

Desha: What are their names?

Damien: I dunno. This is just a generic example.

Desha: Generic is dead, like the people here. Let's bring it to life. You describe them, I'll name them.

Damien: Two of 'em are puppets of wealthy business owners. Th' other two promise to reduce corruption, so they're more popular.

Desha: The names of the first two are Pinoke and Oscar.

Damien: Huh?

Desha: Pinoke is a nickname for Pinnochio, the puppet. And Oscar's last name is the Grouch. He lives in trash just like corrupt politicians.

Damien: OK. Whatever.

Desha: The other two are named Barack, 'cause we can hope for change, and Hypatia 'cause women should rule.

Damien: Wi' these four live candidates, who'd win?

Desha: Either Barack or Hypatia, obviously, 'cause voters want less corruption. They don't want puppets, not Pinoke, not Oscar.

Damien: Right. 'N the voters would get what they want. Bu' then big-money donors arrive.

Desha: So, now you'll say that their money buys advertising that hypnotizes the voters. The way headlights freeze baby rabbits?

Damien: Only th' terminally stupid'd fall for that.

Desha: So tell me why you think money changes who wins.

Damien: Wealthy business owners, who're th' biggest campaign donors, in private they choose which puppet gets their money. If they like th' look of Pinoke's nose, then Oscar's paid t' hike th' Appalachian trail.

Desha: That would make Oscar a grouch. What if he should refuse?

Damien: They point out he'll lose. Without their dollars. 'R they bribe 'im with favors, if that's what it takes.

Desha: A dumpster full of cookies?

Damien: Or a subscription t' Grouch Magazine if that floats his boat. That'd leave Pinoke and Hypatia and Barack. Who'd win that three-way race?

Desha: Ah, lots of voters would split their votes between Barack and Hypatia. That would make it easier for Pinoke to win.

Damien: Plus Cock Brothers 'n Forbes levels of dollars'd pay for ads that attack Hypatia and Barack. Plus news coverage that favors Pinoke. That'd be enough t' rig th' race.

Desha: A puppet wins. That's unfair! More voters prefer either Barack or Hypatia.

Damien: That's what I've learned. Bu' now I'm stuck. I dunno how t' change elections so they don't suck.

Desha: We can call attention to this deception. We'll get voters to concentrate their votes on either Barack or Hypatia.

Damien: Bu' who decides which candidate to dump?

Desha: We the voters could get one to vacate their spot, right?

Damien: Tha' would coax th' wealthiest campaign donors t' quietly fund yet another candidate. One who also promises t' reduce corruption.

Desha: Let's name this one Ghandi. Yes, that tactic's difficult to beat. It splits the voters even more, making it even harder to defeat their single puppet.

Damien: All that happens in primary elections. Then in th' general elections we have to choose between th' two puppets, one from each party.

Desha: Now it makes even more sense why we have just two main political parties.

Damien: Yup. 'N years ago th' puppet politicians passed a law t' give funding t' a third-party presidential candidate who gets more than five percent of th' vote.

Desha: I thought that was a good thing. Now I see it's a bribe that seduces liberals into splitting their votes. While conservatives quietly concentrate their votes on just one candidate.

Damien: You learn fast.

Desha: I have a degree in political science. But they never taught us this.

Damien: Th' unfairness is hidden. It's hidden by numbers, 'n math to me is scary.

Desha: What scares me is that our planet is losing snow leopards and elephants, and rhinoceros ... es, and dolphins, and corral reefs, and plant species that could cure and feed.

Damien: Is tha' what got you into politics?

Desha: At the age of fourteen, ... my mother died. And that's when lots of people, especially my father, lied. They told me everything would turn out fine.

Damien: I can't imagine what 't would be like to lose someone so close. At such a young age.

Desha: I can't either. It's still unreal. Especially because just six years later my brother was killed in the Middle East.

Damien: How did'ya cope?

Desha: I almost lost hope. Then I realized my mother was killed by incompetent city officials. They failed to time pedestrian walk signals to give walkers a head start. Before the green light for cars. And I realized, that basically, my brother was defending profits for oil companies in the Middle East.

Damien: Is tha' why we're in this graveyard?

Desha: My mother and brother are buried over there.

Damien: Ah.

Desha: Over time I traced the local corruption to the state level, then to DC, then to Congress where they write laws so corrupt.

Damien: Yeah, corruption. I wouldn't know how t' define it, bu' I sure see lots of it.

Desha: It's betrayal, bribery, cheating, and coersion, cronyism, deceitfulness, deluding, and deviousness, dishonesty, duplicity, and embezzelment, entrapment, fraud, and graft, misleading, misrepresenting, and monopolizing, palm greasing, payoffs, swindling, and tax evasion, treachery, underhandedness, unethicalness, unscrupulousness, and wickedness. Not to mention lying and littering.

Damien: Littering?

Desha: It's a gateway crime.

Damien: Huh?

Desha: Like not picking up dog poop, 'n not wearing a face mask during an epidemic.

Damien: Your brain is on another wild ride?

Damien: A dog owner doesn't pick up her dog's poop because her dog will poke it with his snout. She picks it up so other dogs don't poke it with their snouts. In return, those other dog owners protect her dog by picking up their dog's poop. It's a poop scooper loop. And the same kind of loop works with face masks during an epidemic.

Damien: 'Kay ....

Desha: In other words, corruption's a situation where someone can get something by not caring about the suffering of others.

Damien: Tha' makes sense.

Desha: I have a tatoo that helps remind me to keep fighting that fight.

Damien: Against people who don't, er, participate in ... super pupper ... pooper scooper loops? C'n I see it? Th' tatoo?

Desha: Fighting against corruption. It's for private tours only.

Damien: What's it say?

Desha: After I realized the real causes of the deaths of my mother and brother, I decided to kick some ... sense into the political world. The tatoo's a reminder to kick ass, and not stop fighting.

Damien: Who're you fighting?

Desha: I've hit another turning point. Originally I thought the enemy was capitalism, and that socialism was the opposite.

Damien: An' it's not?

Desha: It's not that simple. Then I fought against the Republican party. But recently I realized the Democratic party has been infiltrated by big-money donors.

Damien: I too jus' learned th' Republican party isn't th' real enemy.

Desha: My anger is rising. So I need to figure out, and soon, whose butt I should kick.

Damien: I think I've found th' tipping point.

Desha: The place where a little effort can make a huge change?

Damien: Yeah, bu' that path's blocked.

Desha: By what?

Damien: Math. It's blockin' me from figuring out how t' turn th' world right side up.

Desha: I got as far as algebra and trig and statistics without tearing out my hair. What d'you wanna figure out?

Damien: How should votes be counted to get results that're fair?

Desha: I don't know. Bu' I do know someone who might know th' answer.

Damien: C'n he teach us ...

Desha: Wrong gender.

Damien: Who's she?

Desha: She's a math prof.

Damien: Uh professor of math?

Desha: Yes, at the state university. That's where I got my degree.

Damien: I couldn't cope with even commun'ty college. 'N textbooks hold weasely words that try t' drag my brain to useless destinations.

Desha: So you're a loser.

Damien: Huh?

Desha: You're choosing to give up instead of fight. That's losing.

Damien: I'm not brainy. How do I fight against math?

Desha: What took you this far?

Damien: I'm short 'n not handsome. So workin' hard t' earn money is all I've got t' keep my fiancé happy.

Desha: What's so special about her?

Damien: Sh' appreciates me, even though I'm notta hunk surround'd by adoring women.

Desha: All you need is one.

Damien: D' you have someone?

Desha: I have my Willie.

Damien: Whoa! Say what?

Desha: My partner's name is Willie.

Damien: Are you two happy? You and your ... Willie?

Desha: What's your disability? Did you never learn to read? Do the words migrate across the page like a musk ox? Do you nod off on page three and spill your hot cocoa on your ... lap? There's gotta be somethin' more.

Damien: It hurts t' read. Th' words're a pain t' look at.

Desha: Have you tried color-tinted glasses?

Damien: Huh? I don't follow.

Desha: Of course not. Never mind. I'll send you to a website where you can find out more. In the meantime, do you want me to arrange a meeting with this math prof?

Damien: 'K bu' only if she can explain things in spoken words 'n not use lots of jargon, or academic speak.

Desha: I think she can be OK with that. 'N maybe I can translate if that's what you really need.

Damien: It's what I need. What d'you need?

Desha: In my early youth we were so happy then, even though money we lacked. Then the world turned upside down. I saw suffering that I hadn't seen before. And I traced the cause of suffering to corruption in government. Those ... grrr ... politicians are the puppets of greedy money huggers. Now I want to send them to hell, those greedy evil buggers.

Damien: Which ones? Th' politicians or th' big campaign contributors?

Desha: Whoever is most to blame.

Damien: From what I can tell, they're both clueless. About why money works so well as puppet strings. Can we fight against cluelessness?

Desha: If it would help to carry a banner that says End Cluelessness Now I will. I'll fight against whoever's to blame.

Damien: So what's your plan?

Desha: I call on the spirits of the dead here to inspire me to find a way to shine a light on corruption. I want a light that is so bright that even evil money huggers and egotistic puppets cannot fight back. Not with their wealth, not with lawyers, and not through hired workers. I know that light casts out darkness. In that light the path to better times will appear. Until that happens I'll keep on fighting. Fighting with words. Fighting with deeds. Doing whatever it takes to bring good times back to us, the voters, the majority.

Damien: Whatever it takes? Yeah, 't seems it'll take some looking into mathematics.

Desha: I'm willing to help you fight against your fear of math. Let's do this!

Damien: I'm ready to fight! I jus' hope there's no evil fractions. Otherwise I'm ready t' find out how to turn th' world right side up. [better final line?]
