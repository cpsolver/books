Voice Actor Casting Suggestions

These voice descriptions reveal the author's intention for the character voices.  At the end of the list are voice descriptions that apply to all characters.

D: Damien, young adult male, tenor voice.  Slight Puerto Rican accent.  In earlier scenes uses slightly lazy pronunciation (such as lookin' instead of looking) on non-important words.

K: Katerina, young adult female, soprano or alto voice.  Speaks as though raised in an upper middle-class family.

JM: Jordan Morton, middle-age adult male, baritone voice.  Speaks boldly as if wealthy. 

F: Fred Finckhster, older adult male, tenor voice.  Speaks like an experienced politician.

De: Desha, young adult female, alto voice.  U.S. American accent, but voice sounds like racially she is mixed-race with some African-American ancestry in addition to European ancestry.  Speaks strong political opinions.

M: Professor Mata, middle-age female, soprano or alto voice.  Sounds like a university professor.  Clear enunciation is important.

P: Professor Pengaritihas, middle-age male, tenor voice.  Slight Indian accent, as if he is the son of immigrants from India.  (At least pronounces the as thee rather than thuh.)  Sounds like a university professor.  Clear enunciation is important.

W: Whit (pronounced like wit), middle-age male, tenor voice.  Sounds like a wise minister or clergyman.  Racially he sounds like he could be the father of Desha.  Clear enunciation is important.

H: Hawken, middle-age male, bass or baritone voice.  Sounds like a pompous wealthy person.

Additional characteristics for specific characters are revealed in the dialog.

Voice suggestions for all characters:  The voices should be natural (full-throated and conversational), lively (with frequent emotion-based pitch inflections although virtually never in the middle of a syllable, and with word-specific loudness changes), and smiling (comedic, but not funny), without any shouting.  The rhythm should be freeform and improvisational without being specified by written notation or metronome-like timing.  When words rhyme without being at the ends of sentences or phrases, a slight timing or emphasis adjustment can help the rhyming to be heard at least subconsciously.  The voice acting should not be self-conscious.
